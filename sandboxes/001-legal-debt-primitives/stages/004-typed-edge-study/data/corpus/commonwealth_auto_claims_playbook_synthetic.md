# Synthetic Commonwealth Auto Claims Playbook

This file is synthetic sample data for dependency-analysis experiments. It is not legal authority.

## Section 1000. Scope and authority

This playbook governs Kentucky private passenger auto intake, coverage verification, PIP routing, adverse notice handling, and records retention.
It implements Section 304.39.010, Section 304.39.080, Section 304.39.117, Section 304.20.040, and Section 806.39.070.2.

## Section 1010. Kentucky intake classification

The claim intake owner shall classify the matter as first-party PIP, liability, uninsured motorist, proof-card, cancellation, or adverse underwriting.
Classification depends on Section 304.39.030, Section 304.20.020, Section 304.20.040, Section 1020, and Section 1030.

## Section 1020. Basic reparation benefits routing

PIP routing shall verify eligibility under Section 304.39.030, payment duty under Section 304.39.040, and priority of security under Section 304.39.050.
If rejection of tort limitation is alleged, the claim shall move to Section 1040.

## Section 1030. Proof validation and card evidence

Proof validation shall compare the policy record against Section 304.39.117, Section 806.39.070.2, Section 806.39.070.3, and Section 806.39.070.4.
If electronic reporting is unavailable, Section 806.39.070.7 controls the exception path.

## Section 1040. No-fault rejection handling

The adjuster shall confirm the election under Section 304.39.060 and applicant disclosure retention under Section 304.39.120.
If the rejection form is missing, the file shall return to Section 1020.

## Section 1050. Cancellation and nonrenewal hold

Cancellation holds shall compare Section 304.20.040, Section 304.20.050, Section 304.20.060, and Section 304.39.085 before payments are released.
The notice workflow in Section 1090 shall remain open until the hold is cleared.

## Section 1060. Liability limit verification

Liability limit verification shall confirm Section 304.39.110 and uninsured motorist coordination under Section 304.20.020.
If UM exposure exists, the file shall move to Section 1070.

## Section 1070. UM escalation and external authority

UM escalation shall document the connection between Section 304.20.020, Section 304.39.070, and Section 304.39.110.
The analyst may consult NAIC guidance before closing the escalation, but the source must be recorded in Section 1120.

## Section 1080. Form and template update review

Template updates shall reconcile the forms library in Section 806.39.070.8 with the proof validation workflow in Section 1030.
The forms analyst shall review ISO circulars and carrier templates before approving the release.

## Section 1090. Notice workflow

Notice workflow ownership begins when Section 304.20.040, Section 304.20.050, or Section 304.20.320 applies.
Producer communications under Section 1110 must remain synchronized with this workflow.

## Section 1100. Adverse action letter

The adverse action letter shall explain the specific reason required by Section 304.20.320 and the review process in Section 304.20.330.
Privacy content shall be checked against Section 304.20.360 and the evidence packet in Section 1120.

## Section 1110. Producer communications

Producer communications shall use printed card details from Section 806.39.070.3 and notice workflow status from Section 1090.
If the producer sends corrected data, the notice workflow in Section 1090 shall be reopened.

## Section 1120. Evidence packet

The evidence packet shall include source excerpts for Section 304.20.340, proof-card documents from Section 1030, and external authority notes from Section 1070.
Historical checklists still cite Section 9999 and must be retired.

## Section 1200. Applicant disclosure intake

Applicant disclosure intake shall store the no-fault rejection election described in Section 304.39.120 and route missing election evidence to Section 1040.
The intake form shall also notify proof validation under Section 1030.

## Section 1300. Digital proof exception process

Digital proof exceptions arise when Section 806.39.070.5 cannot be satisfied by ordinary electronic card presentation.
The exception owner shall consult Section 1400 before closing the request.

## Section 1400. Printed proof fallback

Printed proof fallback applies when the digital proof exception process in Section 1300 fails.
The fallback record shall be retained under Section 1500.

## Section 1500. Records retention

Records retention shall preserve basic reparation support under Section 304.39.200, adverse notice records under Section 304.20.340, electronic reporting exceptions under Section 806.39.070.7, and supervisory decisions under Section 1600.

## Section 1600. Supervisory review

Supervisory review shall assess whether errors in Section 1050, Section 1090, Section 1100, or Section 1500 are isolated.
Systemic issues shall be escalated to Section 1700.

## Section 1700. Quality audit

Quality audit shall sample files closed under Section 1600 and verify that every dependency path can be traced to source evidence.
The audit may reopen Section 1600 if the trend suggests a control failure.
