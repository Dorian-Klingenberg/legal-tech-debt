# Synthetic Kentucky Motor Vehicle Insurance Code Excerpts

This file is synthetic sample data for dependency-analysis experiments. It borrows Kentucky-style insurance numbering and subject matter, but it is not legal authority.

## Section 304.39.010. Policy and purpose

The motor vehicle reparations subtitle coordinates required security, basic reparation benefits, tort limitations, proof of insurance, and insurer reporting.
The operating rules in this subtitle depend on Section 304.39.020, Section 304.39.080, Section 304.39.100, and Section 304.39.110.

## Section 304.39.020. Definitions for motor vehicle security

- "Motor vehicle insurance policy" means a policy providing security for a personal motor vehicle subject to this subtitle.
- "Personal motor vehicle" means a motor vehicle principally garaged in Kentucky and not insured as a fleet policy.
- "Secured person" means a person entitled to rely on security described in Section 304.39.070.
- "Basic reparation benefits" means first-party benefits administered under Section 304.39.030 and Section 304.39.040.
- "Proof of insurance" means evidence provided under Section 304.39.117 and 806 KAR-style Section 806.39.070.2.
- "No-fault rejection form" means a form used for an election described in Section 304.39.060.
- "Assigned risk policy" means coverage placed through a residual market mechanism.
- "Coverage lapse" means a termination, cancellation, or nonrenewal that creates a reporting event under Section 304.39.085.
- "Courier endorsement" means an endorsement used only for retired courier programs.

## Section 304.39.030. Right to basic reparation benefits

An injured person may claim basic reparation benefits if the accident, vehicle, claimant, and policy satisfy Section 304.39.040 and Section 304.39.050.
Tort limitation review shall also consider Section 304.39.060 before a bodily injury claim is routed to litigation.

## Section 304.39.040. Obligation to pay basic reparation benefits

An obligor shall pay basic reparation benefits in accordance with included coverages described in Section 304.39.100.
The payment decision shall not be finalized until minimum tort liability limits under Section 304.39.110 and retention rules under Section 304.39.200 have been checked.

## Section 304.39.045. Exclusion from coverage by agreement

A named insured exclusion shall be evaluated against the minimum security rule in Section 304.39.080, the liability minimums in Section 304.39.110, and the proof-card data requirements in Section 806.39.070.5.

## Section 304.39.050. Priority of security for payment

Priority of security shall be resolved by first locating the policy providing security under Section 304.39.080 and then confirming required security under Section 304.39.090.
If a coverage lapse is discovered, the file shall be routed under Section 304.39.085.

## Section 304.39.060. Acceptance or rejection of tort limitation

The no-fault rejection workflow affects basic reparation benefits under Section 304.39.030 and applicant disclosures under Section 304.39.120.
If a rejection election cannot be located, the claim system shall treat the claimant as subject to the ordinary security rules in Section 304.39.080.

## Section 304.39.070. Secured person and obligor recovery rights

A secured person relies on security provided under Section 304.39.080 and minimum required security under Section 304.39.090.
Recovery rights shall be coordinated with uninsured motorist coverage under Section 304.20.020.

## Section 304.39.080. Security covering motor vehicle

Security covering a motor vehicle shall satisfy Section 304.39.090, include coverage described in Section 304.39.100, and support proof of insurance under Section 304.39.117.
The insurer's operational proof-card obligations are implemented in Section 806.39.070.2.

## Section 304.39.083. Binder cancellation

Binder cancellation creates a reporting obligation under Section 304.39.085 and shall follow the Department reporting pathway in Section 806.39.070.6.
The policy team shall also review whether the cancelled binder created a gap under Section 304.39.080.

## Section 304.39.085. Termination reporting to the vehicle regulator

Termination, cancellation, and nonrenewal events shall be compared with VIN reporting duties under Section 304.39.087.
Operational reporting shall follow Section 806.39.070.4 and Section 806.39.070.6.

## Section 304.39.087. Vehicle identification number reporting

Vehicle identification number reporting shall support the verification requirements in Section 806.39.070.4.
Legacy batch files that still refer to Section 304.39.888 shall be treated as stale until a reviewer confirms a current source.

## Section 304.39.090. Required security

Required security shall include coverages described in Section 304.39.100 and liability limits described in Section 304.39.110.
The proof of insurance issued for the security shall satisfy Section 304.39.117.

## Section 304.39.100. Included coverages

Included coverages shall support the right to basic reparation benefits in Section 304.39.030, the payment obligation in Section 304.39.040, and uninsured vehicle coordination in Section 304.20.020.

## Section 304.39.110. Required minimum tort liability insurance

Minimum tort liability insurance shall be verified before applying cancellation, nonrenewal, or termination rules in Section 304.20.040.
Uninsured motorist coverage coordination shall follow Section 304.20.020.
The liability review may use NAIC model bulletins if the bulletin year is recorded in the claim note.

## Section 304.39.117. Motor vehicle insurance card

Proof of insurance shall be provided in paper or electronic format and shall be administered under Section 806.39.070.2 and Section 806.39.070.3.
The card must be available to support county clerk and peace officer review described in Section 806.39.070.5.

## Section 304.39.120. Applicant disclosure and no-fault election

Applicant disclosures shall explain the election described in Section 304.39.060 and shall be retained under Section 304.39.200.
The intake workflow in Section 1200 is responsible for storing the applicant's proof of election.

## Section 304.39.200. Records supporting reparations decisions

Records supporting basic reparation benefits shall include proof-card evidence from Section 806.39.070.3, electronic reporting evidence from Section 806.39.070.4, and review packets from Section 1500.
