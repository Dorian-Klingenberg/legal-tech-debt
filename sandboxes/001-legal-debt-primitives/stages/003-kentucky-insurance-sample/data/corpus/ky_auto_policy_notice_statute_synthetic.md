# Synthetic Kentucky Auto Policy Notice Statute Excerpts

This file is synthetic sample data for dependency-analysis experiments. It is not legal authority.

## Section 304.20.020. Uninsured vehicle coverage

Uninsured motorist coverage shall be coordinated with required minimum tort liability insurance under Section 304.39.110 and included coverages under Section 304.39.100.
If a claimant is a secured person, the analyst shall also review Section 304.39.070.

## Section 304.20.040. Cancellation, nonrenewal, or termination of automobile insurance

Cancellation, nonrenewal, and termination notices shall be evaluated under Section 304.20.050 and Section 304.20.060.
Any automobile notice workflow shall coordinate with proof of insurance under Section 304.39.117 and the internal notice queue in Section 1090.

## Section 304.20.050. Nonpayment notice

Nonpayment notices shall satisfy the cancellation rule in Section 304.20.040 and preserve the proof-card audit trail required by Section 304.39.117.
If a binder is involved, Section 304.39.083 shall control the reporting path.

## Section 304.20.060. Material misrepresentation review

Material misrepresentation review shall support cancellation under Section 304.20.040 and notice quality control under Section 304.20.050.
The recordkeeping packet shall be assembled under Section 304.20.340.

## Section 304.20.300. Property and casualty declination purpose

The declination review framework applies to property and casualty notices under Section 304.20.320, Section 304.20.330, Section 304.20.340, and Section 304.20.350.
Automobile liability policies protected by Section 304.20.040 shall be screened before using this framework.

## Section 304.20.320. Specific reason for declination

A declination shall include a specific reason and shall be reviewable under Section 304.20.330.
The adverse action letter template in Section 1100 implements this requirement.

## Section 304.20.330. Review of adverse underwriting action

Review of adverse underwriting action shall reference the original reason required by Section 304.20.320 and the records retained under Section 304.20.340.
The final response shall state whether penalties under Section 304.20.350 remain possible.

## Section 304.20.340. Recordkeeping for notice decisions

The insurer shall retain the adverse action evidence packet described in Section 1120 and the compliance archive described in Section 1500.
Legacy retention maps still cite Section 304.20.999 and should be corrected.

## Section 304.20.350. Penalties and enforcement

Penalties may apply if the insurer fails to support the specific reason required by Section 304.20.320 or the recordkeeping duty in Section 304.20.340.
The supervisory review in Section 1600 shall evaluate whether the defect is isolated or systemic.

## Section 304.20.360. Privacy notice for adverse action

Privacy and adverse action notices may require FCRA coordination when underwriting information is used.
The notice owner shall document the effective version of any FCRA model notice relied upon in Section 1100.
